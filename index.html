Position Warmth Inside Rigor

The closer digital systems move toward cognition, the more carefully they must remain embodied, accountable, and human.

My work studies how intelligent systems meet the body: through voice, motion, attention, trust, and everyday interaction. The goal is not automation for its own sake, but interfaces that support judgment, preserve agency, and feel genuinely usable in human settings.

From smartphone articulation sensing to student trust in generative AI, the throughline is consistent: build technically rigorous systems that still communicate warmth, restraint, and interpretability.
